Isotope Dashboard — Manager's Manual
======================================

This manual is written for managers who use the Isotope Dashboard to monitor
cyclotron production performance.  No technical or operational knowledge is
assumed.  The dashboard is a read-only HTML page: you cannot break anything
by clicking on it.

For technical documentation covering the internal workings of the system,
see MANUAL.txt.

1. What the Dashboard Tells You
=================================

The Isotope Dashboard gives you a live view of cyclotron production performance
across five radioisotopes:

    Ga-67   Gallium-67
    Rb-81   Rubidium-81
    In-111  Indium-111
    Tl-201  Thallium-201
    I-123   Iodine-123

At a glance it answers three management questions:

    1. Are the cyclotrons running efficiently?
       → Efficiency KPI and Within-spec KPI

    2. Are we delivering what was ordered, on time and in full?
       → OTIF KPI and missed-order table

    3. Which shift team is performing best?
       → Ploeg leaderboard

The page refreshes automatically every 60 seconds.  You do not need to press
F5.  The data shown is therefore never more than one minute old during normal
operation.


1.1  How to open the dashboard
--------------------------------
The dashboard is an HTML file stored on a network share.  Open it by:

    • Double-clicking the shortcut on your desktop or the bureau share, or
    • Opening the file directly from:
          X:\Cyclotron Bureau\Productie dashboard.html

If the file does not open, or if the data appears to be many hours old, check
that your network drives (W: and X:) are mapped.  If they are mapped and the
problem persists, contact R.A.F. Sierkstra (SIE, tel. 7062).


1.2  The navigation buttons
----------------------------
At the top of the full dashboard there are four large buttons.  Each opens a
full-screen pop-up panel.  Close any panel with the × in the top-right corner.

    Dosisoverzicht
        Shows the dosis overview image — the radiation dose for each individual
        employee over the past 30 days and past 365 days.

    DM VSM
        Shows the Daily Management VSM table: the daily targetstroom values
        and the weekly OTIF percentage per isotope group.  This is the same
        data that feeds the OTIF gedraaide producties KPI table.

    Rooster en targetvoorraad
        Shows the planning HTML: the shift schedule, planned maintenance
        activities, and the current target stock overview.

    Productieschema
        Shows the production schema HTML: the detailed production plan for
        the current and upcoming periods. #not plan but schedule#


1.3  Two versions of the dashboard
------------------------------------
There are two versions of the dashboard:

    Full dashboard
        Contains all KPI charts, the Gantt chart, per-isotope detail, the
        ploeg leaderboard, and all pop-up panels.  This is the version
        described in this manual and is the one available on the bureau share
        and on the local machine that runs the pipeline.

    Truncated (operator) dashboard
        A lightweight summary — KPI tables and production summary only.
        This version is served to operator workstations on the network share
        for fast loading.  It does not contain the leaderboard or detail
        charts. #not for fast loading but to keep more sensitive info on the management lvl#

As a manager you will normally use the full dashboard.

2. The Colour System
=====================

The entire dashboard uses a single, consistent colour convention.  Once you
know it, you can read any section at a glance.

    GREEN   The value is on target or above.  No action required.

    RED     The value is below the threshold.  Warrants attention.

    GREY    No data is available for that period.  This is normal for
            weeks that have not yet started or for isotopes not produced
            every week.


2.1  What "on target" means per KPI
--------------------------------------
Each KPI has its own threshold for what counts as green:

    Efficiency      Green when the weekly average is at or above the
                    10-week running average.  In other words, the cell is
                    green when this week is at least as good as recent
                    history.

    Within-spec     Green when the weekly percentage is at or above the
                    10-week running average.

    OTIF            Green when the weekly score is 97% or higher.
                    Below 97% the cell turns red regardless of history.


2.2  Colour in the production tables
--------------------------------------
Individual production cells in the weekly summary tables are also coloured
green or red.  For these cells the colour reflects whether that single
production met the quality specification for its isotope (see Section 7).
#it`s not the cell colour but the colour of the number, no cell colours are used throughout the whole HTML#

2.3  Colour in the leaderboard
--------------------------------
Team (ploeg) rankings use a slightly different scale:

    GREEN   The team's score is more than 2 percentage points above the
            average of all teams.

    RED     The team's score is more than 2 percentage points below the
            average of all teams.

    BLACK   The team is within 2 percentage points of the average —
            a neutral result.

3. The Three KPIs: Efficiency, Within-Spec, OTIF
==================================================

The top section of the dashboard shows three KPI tables, stacked one below
the other.  Each covers all five isotopes combined and displays performance
week by week.

Each table has the same layout:
    • One row of coloured cells covering the last 10 weeks, one cell per week.
      Each cell shows the percentage value for that week.
    • Three scalar averages below: all-time average, last year average,
      last 3 months average.
    • The most recent week is on the right; older weeks are to the left.
#coloured cells don`t exist in the html#

3.1  Efficiency — how much of the beam reaches the target?
-----------------------------------------------------------
What it measures:
    Beam transmission efficiency: the ratio of µA measured at the target
    faraday to µA measured at the source faraday, expressed as a percentage.
    A higher percentage means more of the generated beam is reaching the
    target; less is being lost in transit.

In plain language:
    The cyclotron generates a beam at the source.  Not all of it arrives at
    the target — some is scattered or absorbed on the way.  This KPI tracks
    how large that loss is.  A drop in efficiency means the beam is losing
    more current between source and target than usual.

How to read the table:
    Each cell shows the average efficiency percentage for that week.
    A green cell means this week was at or above the 10-week average.
    A red cell means it was below.  An isolated red cell is not a concern;
    two or more consecutive red cells indicate a pattern.
#coloured cells don`t exist in the html#
The scalar averages below the cells show whether the recent dip or rise is a
genuine trend or a short-term fluctuation.  If the last year average and the
all-time average are close, performance is stable.


3.2  Success rate (% Binnen Spec) — are productions meeting quality requirements?
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
What it measures:
    The percentage of completed productions where the beam current at the
    target (or, for some isotopes, the yield) fell within the permitted
    specification window.  100% means every production was within spec.
    Lower numbers mean some productions fell outside the required range.

In plain language:
    Each isotope has a defined acceptable range for its targetstroom (beam
    current at the target).  A production outside that range does not
    necessarily mean the product is rejected, but it indicates the process
    deviated from the target.  Consistent scores below 100% point to a
    systematic issue with a particular isotope or cyclotron.

How to read the table:
    Same layout as Efficiency.  A week with no data (grey) means no
    productions were completed that week — normal for weeks with scheduled
    maintenance or holidays.


3.3  OTIF gedraaide producties — did we deliver the ordered beam current?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
What it measures:
    For each completed production: the ratio of actual targetstroom to the
    nominal (ordered) targetstroom, expressed as a percentage.  100% means
    the nominal was exactly met; above 100% means it was exceeded; below
    100% means the output fell short.  Productions that were not carried out
    at all are excluded from this KPI.

    The pass threshold is 97%.  Cells at or above 97% are green; below
    97% they are red.
#coloured cells don`t exist in the html#
In plain language:
    Of the productions that ran, did the cyclotron reach the ordered output?
    A score below 97% means one or more productions that week fell short of
    the ordered targetstroom.

Note:
    A separate, more detailed OTIF view — covering individual missed orders
    per product — is in Section 6.

4. Reading Trends
==================

A single bad week is not a crisis.  The dashboard is most useful when you
use it to distinguish a one-off deviation from a structural decline.


4.1  Using the weekly cells and averages together
--------------------------------------------------
The 10-week row shows short-term variation.  The scalar averages (10 weeks,
3 months, 12 months) give you the bigger context.

Read them together as follows:

    SCENARIO A — One red cell, rest green, averages stable
        A single bad week surrounded by green cells.  The averages are barely
        affected.  This is likely a one-off event (scheduled maintenance,
        a single failed production, a public holiday reducing output).
#coloured cells don`t exist in the html#
    SCENARIO B — Two or more consecutive red cells
        A run of below-average weeks is a pattern, not noise.  If the
        3-month average is also dropping, the decline started before the
        visible weeks.  The production tables (Section 7) show which isotope
        or shift is involved.

    SCENARIO C — All cells green but the all-time average is higher than last year
        Performance is consistently good now but was better historically.
        The decline happened gradually and is not obvious from the recent
        weeks alone.  The difference between the last year and last 3 months
        averages shows the scale of it.

    SCENARIO D — One or two grey cells (no data)
        Weeks with no productions — typically maintenance shutdowns or
        holidays.  These are expected and do not affect the averages.


4.2  Signal thresholds
-----------------------
    Two or more consecutive red cells in any KPI  indicates a pattern,
                                                  not a one-off.

    All-time average more than 5% above           indicates a gradual
    last year average                             structural decline.

    OTIF below 90% in any single week             indicates a significant
                                                  event that week.


4.3  Comparing KPIs to each other
------------------------------------
The three KPIs measure different aspects of the same production process.
Looking at them side by side can pinpoint the root cause:

    Efficiency down, Within-spec stable
        More beam is being lost between source and target, but the
        targetstroom still falls within spec.  Possible cause: transmission
        losses increasing (optics, steering, vacuum). #tuning can be a cause here too#

    Within-spec down, Efficiency stable
        Beam transmission is normal but the targetstroom is drifting outside
        the allowed spec range.  Possible cause: cyclotron tuning or
        operator setting.

    OTIF down, both others stable
        The cyclotron is running well but did not deliver the ordered current
        level.  Could indicate scheduling pressure or a short-notice capacity
        reduction. #could indicate PROC errors#

    All three down simultaneously
        A broader systemic issue — equipment fault, key staff absence, or a
        process change that affected all parameters. #don`t mention staff absence#

5. Ploeg Leaderboard: Team Performance
========================================

The ploeg leaderboard ranks the five shift teams by the percentage of
productions they oversaw that were within specification.  It shows not just
how the facility is doing overall, but which teams are above and which are
below the group average.


5.1  What is a ploeg?
----------------------
The cyclotron runs 24 hours a day, seven days a week, split into three shifts:

    Ochtenddienst   Morning shift    07:00 – 15:00
    Middagdienst    Afternoon shift  15:00 – 23:00
    Nachtdienst     Night shift      23:00 – 07:00

Five rotating teams (ploegen, numbered 1–5) cover these shifts on a rolling
schedule.  Each production run is attributed to the shift team that operated
the cyclotron during that run.  If a production spans two shifts, credit is
split proportionally between the two teams.


5.2  The leaderboard table
----------------------------
The leaderboard ranks each team by their within-spec percentage over the
trailing 30 days.  The colour coding follows the convention described in
Section 2.3 (green = more than 2% above the inter-team average, red = more
than 2% below, black = within 2%).

To the right of the ranking, three summary columns show performance over
longer fixed periods: last 1 month, last 3 months, and last 6 months.  Use
these to tell apart a team that has recently improved from one that has been
consistently strong over time.


5.3  The rolling-average trend chart
--------------------------------------
Below the table is a line chart showing each team's 30-day rolling average
over the past six months.  Each team has its own coloured line.

How to read it as a manager:
    • A line trending upward indicates improving performance.
    • A line trending downward indicates declining performance.
    • Lines that are closely grouped indicate consistent parity between teams.
    • A single line pulling away from the rest (up or down) indicates one
      team performing notably differently — worth understanding why.


5.4  Monthly winner banner
----------------------------
At the top of the leaderboard section a banner highlights the team with the
highest within-spec percentage in the most recently completed calendar month.
This is intended as a recognition signal and a quick reference for monthly
performance reviews.


5.5  Viewing a team's individual productions
---------------------------------------------
Clicking on a team row in the leaderboard opens a detail panel (pop-up)
showing the individual productions attributed to that team over the selected
period.  You can filter by period (1 month, 3 months, 6 months) using the
buttons at the top of the panel.

Each row shows the isotope, date, BO number, and whether the production was
within spec.  This lets you see whether a team's low score is driven by one
isotope or by a broader pattern across all products.

Close the panel by clicking the X in the top-right corner.

6. OTIF Cyclotron: Missed Orders
==================================

Further down the dashboard there is a dedicated OTIF Cyclotron section.
This goes deeper than the OTIF KPI table (Section 3.3): instead of a
weekly average percentage, it shows exactly which product orders were missed,
in which week, and why.


6.1  The missed-order table
-----------------------------
The table lists missed or incomplete orders from the OTIF tracking workbook.
Each row contains:

    Week        The ISO week in which the miss occurred (format: YYYYwWW,
                e.g. 2024w12).
    Product     The isotope product that was short-delivered:
                Gallium, I-123, Indium, Krypton, Thallium, or Rubidium.
    Count       The number of orders missed for that product in that week.
    Reason      The recorded cause of the miss as entered in the workbook.


6.2  Per-product KPI line charts
----------------------------------
Below the missed-order table there are six line charts, one per product
(Gallium, I-123, Indium, Krypton, Thallium, Rubidium/Krypton).

Each chart shows the weekly OTIF percentage for that product over up to the
last 52 weeks.  This allows you to see whether a product has a chronic
delivery problem or whether misses are isolated events.

How to read them:
    • A line holding steady near 100% → no structural issue.
    • A line with recurring dips → investigate whether the dips correlate
      with a specific reason code (see table above).
    • A line that has dropped and stayed low → the product may have a
      systematic supply or process problem requiring management attention.

7. Production Summary Tables (This Week / Last Week)
======================================================

Below the KPI section the dashboard shows two production summary tables:
one for the current week and one for the previous week.  These give you a
row-by-row view of every completed production run, allowing you to see at
a glance where within-spec issues occurred.


7.1  Table layout
------------------
Each table has one row per production entry.  The table has six columns:

    Gallium (µA)              Targetstroom for Ga-67
    Rubidium (% + µA)         Efficiency percentage and beam current for Rb-81
    Indium (µA)               Targetstroom for In-111
    Thallium 1.2 (µA)         Targetstroom for Tl-201 on kant 1.2
    Thallium 2.1 (µA)         Targetstroom for Tl-201 on kant 2.1
    Iodine (Yield%/Output% + µA)   Yield percentage, output percentage, and
                                   beam current for I-123

Thallium is split into two columns because it can run simultaneously on both
beam ports.  If one isotope has more productions in a week than another, the
shorter column fills with empty cells.


7.2  Reading cell colours
--------------------------
Cell colours follow the same green/red convention as the rest of the
dashboard, but they reflect individual production quality rather than
weekly averages:
#coloured cells don`t exist in the html#
    Gallium, Indium, Thallium 1.2, Thallium 2.1
        Green   Targetstroom was within the allowed specification range.
        Red     Targetstroom was outside the specification range.

    Rb-81
        Each cell shows two values: the efficiency percentage and the beam
        current in µA.  The cell is green when the efficiency (the ratio of
        measured to ordered activity) is within specification.

    I-123
        Each cell shows the yield percentage and the output percentage.
        Both values must be within their respective ranges for the cell to
        show green.  A red cell means one or both values were out of spec.


7.3  Opening the Production History panel
------------------------------------------
Each production cell contains a BO number (batch order number) shown as a
clickable link.  Clicking it opens the Production History panel.  This shows
the shift-level breakdown for that specific production run: which shift was
responsible, the exact timing, and the full BO number.

This is particularly useful when a production is out of spec and you want
to know which shift team was on duty — you can then cross-reference with
the ploeg leaderboard (Section 5).

Close the panel with the X button.


7.4  What to look for as a manager
-------------------------------------
    • A column that is mostly red in both the current and previous week
      indicates a persistent issue with that isotope.

    • Scattered red cells across multiple isotopes and days suggest a
      broader process or cyclotron issue rather than an isotope-specific one.

    • A completely empty column for the week means that isotope was not
      scheduled for production — this is normal for some products on some
      weeks.

8. The Gantt Chart
===================

The Gantt chart is a visual timeline showing cyclotron productions over the
last several days and the coming days.  It is primarily an operational tool
for shift leads, but managers can use it for a quick sanity-check of whether
production is running as planned.


8.1  What the chart shows
--------------------------
The chart displays three lanes, one per cyclotron position:

    Kant 1      One side of the IBA cyclotron
    Kant 2      The other side of the IBA cyclotron
    Philips     The Philips cyclotron

Each coloured block represents one production run.  The block's horizontal
position shows when the run started and ended.  A red vertical line labelled
"Nu" (Now) marks the current time, so you can immediately see what is
running right now and what has recently finished.


8.2  Colour key
----------------
Note: in the Gantt chart colours identify isotopes, not performance.
Green here means I-123, not "on target" as it does in the KPI tables.

    Blue    Ga-67   (Gallium-67)
    Red     Rb-81   (Rubidium-81)
    Cyan    In-111  (Indium-111)
    Yellow  Tl-201  (Thallium-201)
    Green   I-123   (Iodine-123)
    Grey    Pauze   (scheduled gap / no production)


8.3  Navigating the chart
--------------------------
    Scroll left/right   Use the horizontal scrollbar at the bottom of the
                        chart to move through time.

    Jump to Now         Click the "Spring naar Nu" button above the chart
                        to centre the view on the current time.  This is
                        the fastest way to see what is running right now.

    Hover tooltip       Hovering over any production block shows a tooltip
                        with the BO number, product name, start and end
                        times, and duration.


8.4  Planned vs actual
-----------------------
The chart merges two data sources:

    Planned entries     Loaded from the cyclotron production schedule.
                        Shown if the actual production record is not yet
                        available.

    Actual entries      Once a production is completed and recorded, the
                        actual record replaces the planned entry.  Actual
                        records take priority.

As a manager, if you see a production block that should have started but
is absent or appears very short, it may indicate a delayed or aborted run.
This would also show up as a low OTIF score for that week.

9. Reference: When Something Looks Wrong
=========================================

This section lists the most common situations and what they usually indicate.


9.1  Dashboard issues (page / display)
----------------------------------------

    The page is not refreshing automatically
        The dashboard refreshes every 60 seconds by default.  If it stops:
        • Press F5 to reload manually.
        • Check that your browser is not in an offline mode.
        • If the file cannot be found, verify that the X: drive is mapped.

    The data looks many hours old
        If the values on the page appear unchanged across multiple manual
        refreshes (F5) during working hours, the pipeline has likely stopped.
        → Contact R.A.F. Sierkstra (SIE, tel. 7062).

    A red banner reading "FILE TAMPERED" appears at the top of the page
        The HTML file was edited outside the automated pipeline.  This is
        detected by a checksum comparison and is shown as a warning.
        → Do not edit the HTML file manually.  Contact R.A.F. Sierkstra
          (SIE, tel. 7062).  The banner disappears on the next automated
          update.

    Sections are missing (e.g. leaderboard is blank, planning panel is empty)
        The pipeline depends on several network files (Excel workbooks,
        shared drives).  If one of those files is unavailable, the
        corresponding section will be absent or show a warning message.
        → Check that the W: drive is mapped.  If it is, contact
          R.A.F. Sierkstra (SIE, tel. 7062) — the source file may have
          moved or been renamed.


9.2  KPI concerns
-------------------

    Two or more consecutive red cells in any KPI
        Indicates a pattern.  The missed-order table (Section 6) and the
        production summary tables (Section 7) show which isotope or shift
        is involved.

    OTIF below 90% in any single week
        Indicates a significant unplanned event that week.  The reason
        column in the missed-order table (Section 6) shows the cause.

    Efficiency and within-spec are both low simultaneously
        Points to a cyclotron or process issue rather than a scheduling
        or data problem.


9.3  Ploeg / team concerns
----------------------------

    One team is consistently red for more than four weeks
        The per-ploeg detail panel (Section 5.5) shows which isotope or
        shift time is pulling the score down.

    All teams are red simultaneously
        When every team's score drops at the same time the cause is not
        team-specific.  It points to a process or equipment issue.


9.4  Contact
--------------
    Pipeline / dashboard issues (data not         R.A.F. Sierkstra
    updating, missing sections, FILE TAMPERED)    SIE, tel. 7062

    Production and equipment matters              Operators-technicians
                                                  on shift

    Supply-related misses (Bulk shortage)         Supply chain /
                                                  procurement

10. Quick-Reference Card
=========================

Print or bookmark this section for use during review meetings.


10.1  Colour meanings at a glance
-----------------------------------
    GREEN    On target or above.      No action needed.
    RED      Below threshold.         Warrants attention or discussion.
    GREY     No data for this period. Normal for unscheduled weeks.


10.2  KPI definitions (one line each)
---------------------------------------
    Efficiency     Beam transmission efficiency: µA at target faraday as a
                   percentage of µA at source faraday.  Higher = less beam
                   lost between source and target.

    Within-spec    % of productions where beam current (or yield) was
                   within the allowed quality window.  Target: as close to
                   100% as possible.

    OTIF           % of productions where the delivered beam current met or
                   exceeded the ordered level.  Pass threshold: 97%.


10.3  KPI thresholds that trigger red
---------------------------------------
    Efficiency     Below the 10-week running average for that week.
    Within-spec    Below the 10-week running average for that week.
    OTIF           Below 97% for that week.
    Leaderboard    More than 2 percentage points below the team average.


10.4  Specification limits per isotope (within-spec check)
------------------------------------------------------------
    Ga-67   Philips cyclotron:   75 – 85 µA beam current
            IBA cyclotron:      130 – 140 µA beam current
    In-111  Philips cyclotron:   75 – 85 µA beam current
            IBA cyclotron:      130 – 140 µA beam current
    Tl-201  166 – 174 µA beam current
    Rb-81   95 – 105% efficiency (measured vs ordered activity)
    I-123   Output:      78 – 114.9% of expected yield
            Beam current: 96 – 124 µA
