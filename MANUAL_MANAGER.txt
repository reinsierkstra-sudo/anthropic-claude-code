Isotope Dashboard — Manager's Manual
======================================

This manual is written for managers who use the Isotope Dashboard to monitor
cyclotron production performance.  No technical or operational knowledge is
assumed.  The dashboard is a read-only HTML page: you cannot break anything
by clicking on it.

For technical documentation covering the internal workings of the system,
see MANUAL.txt.

1. What the Dashboard Tells You
=================================

The Isotope Dashboard gives you a live view of cyclotron production performance
across five radioisotopes:

    Ga-67   Gallium-67
    Rb-82   Rubidium-82
    In-111  Indium-111
    Tl-201  Thallium-201
    I-123   Iodine-123

At a glance it answers three management questions:

    1. Are the cyclotrons running efficiently?
       → Efficiency KPI and Within-spec KPI

    2. Are we delivering what was ordered, on time and in full?
       → OTIF KPI and missed-order table

    3. Which shift team is performing best?
       → Ploeg leaderboard

The page refreshes automatically every 60 seconds.  You do not need to press
F5.  The data shown is therefore never more than one minute old during normal
operation.


1.1  How to open the dashboard
--------------------------------
The dashboard is an HTML file stored on a network share.  Open it by:

    • Double-clicking the shortcut on your desktop or the bureau share, or
    • Opening the file directly from:
          X:\Cyclotron Bureau\Productie dashboard.html

If the file does not open, or if the data appears to be many hours old, check
that your network drives (W: and X:) are mapped.  If they are mapped and the
problem persists, contact the system owner (see Section 9).


1.2  Two versions of the dashboard
------------------------------------
There are two versions of the dashboard:

    Full dashboard
        Contains all KPI charts, the Gantt chart, per-isotope detail, the
        ploeg leaderboard, and all pop-up panels.  This is the version
        described in this manual and is the one available on the bureau share
        and on the local machine that runs the pipeline.

    Truncated (operator) dashboard
        A lightweight summary — KPI tables and production summary only.
        This version is served to operator workstations on the network share
        for fast loading.  It does not contain the leaderboard or detail
        charts.

As a manager you will normally use the full dashboard.

2. The Colour System
=====================

The entire dashboard uses a single, consistent colour convention.  Once you
know it, you can read any section at a glance.

    GREEN   The value is on target or above.  No action required.

    RED     The value is below the threshold.  Warrants attention.

    GREY    No data is available for that period.  This is normal for
            weeks that have not yet started or for isotopes not produced
            every week.


2.1  What "on target" means per KPI
--------------------------------------
Each KPI has its own threshold for what counts as green:

    Efficiency      Green when the weekly average is at or above the
                    10-week running average.  In other words, the bar is
                    green when this week is at least as good as recent
                    history.

    Within-spec     Green when the weekly percentage is at or above the
                    10-week running average.

    OTIF            Green when the weekly score is 97% or higher.
                    Below 97% the bar turns red regardless of history.


2.2  Colour in the production tables
--------------------------------------
Individual production cells in the weekly summary tables are also coloured
green or red.  For these cells the colour reflects whether that single
production met the quality specification for its isotope (see Section 7).


2.3  Colour in the leaderboard
--------------------------------
Team (ploeg) rankings use a slightly different scale:

    GREEN   The team's score is more than 2 percentage points above the
            average of all teams.

    RED     The team's score is more than 2 percentage points below the
            average of all teams.

    BLACK   The team is within 2 percentage points of the average —
            a neutral result.

3. The Three KPIs: Efficiency, Within-Spec, OTIF
==================================================

The top section of the dashboard shows three KPI tables side by side.  Each
covers all five isotopes combined and displays performance week by week.

Each table has the same layout:
    • A row of coloured bars, one per week, covering the last 10 weeks.
    • Three scalar averages to the right: last 10 weeks, last 3 months,
      last 12 months.
    • The most recent week is on the right; older weeks are to the left.


3.1  Efficiency — are we getting the most out of beam time?
------------------------------------------------------------
What it measures:
    How much radioactive product (in millicuries) is produced for every
    micro-ampere-hour (µAh) of beam charge delivered by the cyclotron.
    A higher number means more product from the same amount of beam time.

In plain language:
    Think of it as miles per gallon for the cyclotron.  If the number goes
    up, we are getting more product from the same running cost.  If it drops,
    something is reducing yield — a degraded target, beam losses, or a
    process issue.

How to read the chart:
    Each bar is the average efficiency across all productions in that week.
    A green bar means this week was at or above the 10-week average.
    A red bar means it was below.  An isolated red bar is not a concern; two
    or more consecutive red bars warrant a conversation with the team.

The scalar averages (right side of the table) show whether the recent dip or
rise is a genuine trend or a short-term fluctuation.  If the 10-week average
and the 12-month average are close, performance is stable.


3.2  Within-Spec — are productions meeting quality requirements?
-----------------------------------------------------------------
What it measures:
    The percentage of completed productions where the beam current (or, for
    some isotopes, the yield) fell within the permitted specification window.
    100% means every production was within spec.  Lower numbers mean some
    productions fell outside the required range.

In plain language:
    Each isotope has a defined acceptable range for its production parameters.
    A production outside that range does not necessarily mean the product is
    rejected, but it does indicate the process deviated from the target.
    Consistent scores below 100% suggest a systematic issue with a particular
    isotope or cyclotron.

How to read the chart:
    Same bar layout as Efficiency.  A week with no data (all grey) means no
    productions were completed that week — normal for weeks with scheduled
    maintenance or holidays.


3.3  OTIF — did we deliver the ordered beam current?
------------------------------------------------------
What it measures:
    OTIF stands for On Time In Full.  Here it specifically tracks whether
    the actual beam current delivered during each production was equal to or
    above the nominal (ordered) target current.  The result is expressed as a
    percentage: 100% means the target was exactly met; above 100% means it
    was exceeded; below 100% means it fell short.

    The pass threshold is 97%.  Bars at or above 97% are green; below
    97% they are red.

In plain language:
    Did the cyclotron deliver the power level that was requested?  A score
    below 97% means the beam current was not sufficient during one or more
    productions that week, which can affect product yield and customer orders.

Note:
    A separate, more detailed OTIF view — covering individual missed customer
    orders per product — is in Section 6.
